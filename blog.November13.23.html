November 13

For the first part of the day, I want to finish the assignment for the Political Science class. I'm so reluctant to do that, but hopefully, It won't take long to write, and I will be left with plenty of time to finish yesterday's code.

I feel like I'm wasting time reading about this stuff, to make matters worse, someone who sat beside me, said that it's useless to improve writing skills as an engineer. I have mixed feelings about this, as a non-native person, my level of literacy for English is akin to high schooler or even worse, so naturally, I want to improve on that. But, we have finite time and we need to choose carefully what to spend time improving on. So, the question is whether it's more important than doing what I've done yesterday, for example. On the other hand, people naturally tend to dismiss bad-expressed ideas with a person who expressed this idea to add on and everything he will say afterward. I think writing skills are worth pursuing for 5% of my monthly effort(this blog should suffice).

I don't know how to not be awkward when speaking with someone. Usage of slang doesn't come naturally to me. It might be reasonable to assume that some part of it comes due to my terrible English, but it was actually the same when I was speaking in my native language, back in my home country. I wonder why it takes me longer to get closer to a person compared to others. The only person with whom I could talk without restraint was my friend from my home country, but it took me 2 years to reach the level where I could speak with him casually without thinking what should I say afterward.

I also got WHIMIS tests to be able to do something in the shop. Shop orientation will be held tomorrow and afterward, I'll be able to attend different workshops on machining, etc..

The US Electoral College is the most confusing system EVER. I bet if they made the process easier they would've increased the turn-out rate by a high margin. Even Chatgpt is confused with the two-meaning word "electorate". It's 3 pm and I'm not even 1/5th done with this essay.

People track something that they care about and want to improve on. I believe to control one's time, it's necessary to track it, up to the minute. But, as with everything of that sort, it's a very mundane activity and usually people don't see its benefits when everything is going well. That is the case for me as well. I used track time and gave up on it for 5 times this year. And, usually, It wasn't longer than a week for me to lose motivation or to just forget to do it. I'll give it another try starting from now on.

I wonder when one can be sure that his idea is novel. There are too many people in the world to say that something is novel.

6 pm: I'm half finished but I want to quit. I'll go buy some cookies with milk and give myself a little rest. (I'm weak, sorry)

Fast forward to 10 pm, I didn't finish this paper, and that WHIMIS test completely went out of my head. But, It's already 10 pm and I don't want to jeopardize my beloved 7 am regime just to do some work with my messed up foggy brain.

To conclude:

This day was shit. 3/10.
